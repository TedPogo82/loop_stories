Loop Stories Presents:

The Dionysus Treasure

by Ted Pogo

Story and website made by Ted Pogo


Theatre of Dionysus

Athens, Greece

The shadows of the Dionysus hides a vast series of underground ruins recently discovered. The mapping of the surface ruins have attracted many things from tourists to vandals. However, the ruins go deeper than discovered, and legends tell of an ancient and priceless artifact that exists at the bottom.

You are on vacation with your wife, part of the Dionysus tour. The underground ruins travel below the theatre of Dionysus, snaking along various attractions. Stanchions prevent tourists from venturing off the path, but the intrigue of mysterious tunnels capture the curious. You and your wife drag behind the tour group and manage to explore one of the “forbidden” tunnels, grabbing a torch along the way.

As you delve deeper into the untapped ruins, you discover an entire world left out of the tour. The path is surrounded by mysterious statues of old Greek gods, but also deep holes that drop into pure darkness. You two watch your steps carefully as you curiously venture further.

Alone in a timeless world, you suddenly hear voices. They aren’t part of the tour group.

“Hey!” says a grim voice from behind.

You and your wife turn around to see a small group of thieves standing not more than 10 feet away. In the center of the group is a man with a fedora, a pistol by his side, and a scroll drooping from his hand. He develops a sinister smile and steps forward, rolling his treasure map up.

You stand in front of your wife. “Um.. sorry,” you say. “we were just heading back to the tour group-”

The man with the fedora draws his pistol on you and replies. “You ain’t going nowhere. I know you two have come down here for the treasure!”

“No, sir, we just got lost, and-”

WHAM! He hits you with the butt of his pistol, knocking you to the ground. As you recover, you hear your wife’s screams and witness the men taking her. One of them presses his foot on your chest while the others carry her deeper into the ruins.

“She’ll be our… canary!” says the leader as he ushers his group down a corridor. They disappear, leaving the lone thief to finish you off.

The man pressing his foot on your chest looks away at the group for a quick moment, leaving you a chance to do something. You want to grab the knife from your belt, but it is pressed against the ground. However, next to you is a large rock. You grab the rock and swing it across just as he looks back at you, smashing his foot. CRACK! The rock lands squarely on his toes, and he immediately steps off. He stumbles back and accidentally falls over the railing, plunging into the depths of the hole.

“AAAaagggghhh…” His screams fade before he hits the bottom.

You wipe the blood from your forehead and dust your clothes off. Desperate to get your wife back, you head towards the corridor in which the group disappeared. As you round the corner, the path splits. You grab the nearby torch and examine each corridor.

The left path leads to a brightly lit corridor. Looking down the shaft, you see a bright room with gold statues.

The right path is dark and filled with cobwebs.
