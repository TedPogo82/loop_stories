You choose the dark corridor with cobwebs!

Not sure about the cobwebs and the darkness, your gut still tells you this is the right path. You clutch your torch with both hands and slowly creep down the tunnel. Spiders crawl into the small cracks of the cavernous tunnel at your approach, and despair begins to find its way into the recesses of your mind. Before you completely lose hope, you find your wife’s hair claw on the ground!

With a flicker of hope, you press on, continuing down the dark corridor. Eventually, you emerge to a cavern with a waterfall, dropping down past the walkway into a deep dark pool. As you proceed past the waterfall, you see a wooden door on the other side of the cavern. Two ornate sconces are mounted on both sides of the door, illuminating the end of the room. You are sure the theives went through this door, as it is the only exit.

As you near the door, you notice a sign nailed to it. Click! Before the door, you stepped on a small stone platform that activated a mechanism somewhere in the wall. You look around any immediate danger, but whatever trap has sprung seems to be either malfunctioned, or hasn’t presented itself yet. Still, you remain cautious. You read the sign.

“What has one voice but goes on four legs in the morning, two in the afternoon, and three in the evening?” Below the riddle are three stone buttons with pictures. You are certain the wrong choice will spell disaster.
